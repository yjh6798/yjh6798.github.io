<h2 style="font-size: 2.0rem; font-weight: 700; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0.4rem;">
Nanomedicine
</h2>

<hr style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:1.4rem; border:0; border-top:1px solid #d9d9d9;">

<p>
My work in nanomedicine centers on functional nanoparticles and bioactive material interfaces for imaging, sensing, and therapy. I have developed biodegradable silica-based nanoparticles for photoacoustic and fluorescence imaging, afterglow nanosensors for disease monitoring, and surface-engineered nanomaterials for immunoassays and cell–material interactions. I am interested in how nanoscale chemistry, structure, and surface properties influence biological interactions and therapeutic performance.
</p>

<h2 style="font-size: 2.0rem; font-weight: 700; margin-bottom: 0.4rem;">
Medical Micro/Nanorobots
</h2>

<hr style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:1.4rem; border:0; border-top:1px solid #d9d9d9;">

<p>
I develop medical micro/nanorobots for active transport, targeted delivery, and minimally invasive therapy. My current work focuses on nanorobotic systems that can overcome biological barriers such as the blood–brain barrier, dense extracellular matrices, and restricted tissue interfaces that limit conventional drug delivery. I am particularly interested in propulsion, environmental responsiveness, and imaging-guided control inside living tissues.
</p>

<p>
My recent projects include photothermally gated nanorobots for active brain delivery, ultrasound-propelled hydrogel microrobots, and microbubble-based robotic systems for cancer therapy. These projects combine materials design, actuation, imaging, and biological validation to improve therapeutic delivery in complex biological environments.
</p>

<h2 style="font-size: 2.0rem; font-weight: 700; margin-bottom: 0.4rem;">
In Vivo Biofabrication and Acoustic Printing
</h2>

<hr style="margin-top:0; margin-bottom:1.4rem; border:0; border-top:1px solid #d9d9d9;">

<p>
I also work on imaging-guided in vivo biofabrication, with a focus on acoustic printing inside living systems. This research uses focused ultrasound, responsive bioinks, and real-time imaging to form functional materials at target locations without open surgery. The goal is to build minimally invasive platforms that can pattern biomaterials in deep, moving, and structurally complex tissues.
</p>

<p>
My interests in this area include ultrasound-triggered gelation, printable bioink design, motion compensation, and AI-guided control of printing patterns. In the long term, I aim to develop in vivo biofabrication strategies for therapeutic biointerfaces, tissue repair, and regenerative medicine.
</p>
